# IPOReady 3-Year Go-to-Market Strategy

**Period:** July 2026 - June 2029  
**Status:** Phase 1 MVP Launch Planning  
**Last Updated:** June 2026

---

## Executive Summary

IPOReady targets the $30-60M serviceable addressable market (SAM) for IPO readiness software, capturing companies preparing for public markets. Our 3-year strategy pursues 300 customers and $30M ARR through three parallel execution tracks: direct enterprise sales, strategic partnerships with investment bankers and Big 4 firms, and thought leadership content marketing.

**Year 1 Target:** 50 customers, $5M ARR  
**Year 2 Target:** 150 customers, $15M ARR  
**Year 3 Target:** 300 customers, $30M ARR

---

## Market Analysis

### Total Addressable Market (TAM)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| US IPOs per year (historical avg) | 150-300 |
| Average company pre-IPO valuation | $5-100B |
| Decision-makers per company | 3 (CEO, CFO, GC) |
| 3-year universe | 600-900 companies |
| Average contract value (ACV) | $50K-150K |
| **3-Year TAM** | **$30-135M** |

**TAM Notes:**
- IPO volume is cyclical; 2021-2022 saw 300+ IPOs/year; 2024-2025 saw 100-150/year
- Decision-makers span finance, legal, and executive leadership
- ACV varies: smaller IPOs ($500M-$1B) pay $50K; mega IPOs ($10B+) pay $150K+

### Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)

**Target Vertical:** Tech/SaaS companies (70% of all IPOs, highest willingness to pay)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Tech/SaaS IPOs per year (70% of total) | 100-200 |
| 3-year population | 300-600 companies |
| Average ACV (premium tier) | $100K |
| **3-Year SAM** | **$30-60M** |

**SAM Focus:**
- Tech/SaaS companies have higher software budgets and greater regulatory complexity
- These companies operate at scale (usually $1B+ valuation at IPO)
- Higher urgency: tech IPOs face compounded SOX, climate disclosure, and SEC scrutiny
- Faster sales cycles with sophisticated procurement

### Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM)

**Conservative 3-Year Capture Rate: 1-2% of SAM**

| Year | Customer Target | ACV | ARR Target | Market Capture |
|------|-----------------|-----|-----------|-----------------|
| Year 1 | 50 companies | $100K | $5M | 0.25% |
| Year 2 | 150 companies | $100K | $15M | 0.75% |
| Year 3 | 300 companies | $100K | $30M | 1.5% |

**Growth Assumptions:**
- Year 1→2: 3x customer growth (market awareness phase)
- Year 2→3: 2x customer growth (partnerships + category leadership)
- Net retention: 90% (some companies graduate post-IPO; offset by new IPO pipeline)

**Content Marketing - "The IPO Readiness Authority"**

  - Editorial calendar: 6 categories (Regulatory, Strategy, Technology, Case Studies, Tools, Founder Lessons)
  - SEO goal: #1 ranking for 10 primary keywords by end of Year 1
  - Expected monthly organic traffic: 10K→50K by month 18

  - "Lessons from 100 Recent IPOs: What Worked, What Didn't"
  - "SOX Compliance 101: Your 12-Month Roadmap"
  - "Investor Expectations: The Unspoken Requirements"
  - "Post-IPO Compliance: Beyond the Opening Bell"
  - Expected registrants: 500→2,000 per webinar by Year 2

- **Thought Leadership Program**
  - 12-month guest appearance strategy with industry publications (Harvard Business Review, CFO Magazine, TechCrunch)
  - Target: 6 major bylines in Year 1; CEO speaking at 3 industry conferences

- **Email Nurture Campaigns**
  - Segmented by role: CFO track, GC track, CEO track, COO track
  - Funnel: IPO readiness self-assessment → regulatory guide → platform demo

**Expected Inbound Results (Year 1):**
- Content-driven leads: 200-300/month by month 12
- Cost per lead: $50-100 (organic)
- Lead-to-SQL conversion: 15-20%
- Expected inbound deals: 5-10/quarter

#### Outbound Motion

**Target Company Research & Prospecting**

- **IEO (IPO Expectancy Outlook) Identification**
  - Monitor: Bloomberg IPO pipeline, Crunchbase funding rounds ($500M+ Series D/E), venture databases, investor roundtables
  - Identify 500 target companies fitting profile: $1-10B valuation, Series D/E stage, tech/SaaS
  - Build tiered outreach list: Tier 1 (highest probability), Tier 2, Tier 3

- **Cold Outreach Campaign Structure**
  - Sequence: 7-touch outreach mix (email, LinkedIn, phone, event invitation)
  - Personalization: Specific regulatory triggers ("Your Series E round likely triggered SOX Phase 1 planning")
  - Timing: Begin outreach 12-18 months pre-rumored IPO
  - Expected response rate: 3-5% (opens), 0.5-1% (replies), 0.1-0.25% (meetings)

- **Sales Development Workflow (SDR + AE)**
  - SDR: Dials 40 calls/day, targets Tier 1 companies, books ~3-5 meetings/week
  - AE: Closes 5-7 meetings/week into demos; typical close rate 20-30% over 8-12 week cycle

**Expected Outbound Results (Year 1):**
- Outbound-sourced meetings: 12-15/month by month 6; 30-40/month by month 12
- Conversion rate (meeting → customer): 15-20%
- Expected deals from outbound: 15-20 in Year 1

#### Sales Cycle & Process

| Stage | Duration | Key Activities |
|-------|----------|-----------------|
| Discovery | 1-2 weeks | Understand IPO timeline, regulatory complexity, tech stack |
| Evaluation | 3-4 weeks | Demo, security audit (SOC 2), reference calls |
| Trial | 2-4 weeks | Pilot with 1-2 departments (finance + legal) |
| Negotiation | 2-3 weeks | Contract review, terms, deployment plan |
| **Total Cycle** | **8-13 weeks** | Tied to company's IPO timeline |

**Typical Deal Structure (Year 1-2):**
- License fee: $100K/year (fixed)
- Optional add-ons: Advanced compliance module (+$25K), integration services (+$10-30K)
- Term: 1-3 years (longer terms for higher ACV)
- Payment: Annual prepay (50% upfront) or quarterly

## Risk Mitigation & Contingency Plans

### Risk 1: Market Timing / IPO Cycle Downturn

**Risk:** If IPO market cools, fewer companies will initiate IPO process; reduced market demand.

**Mitigation:**
- Diversify customer base: Year 2, begin targeting non-tech IPOs + private equity exits
- Recurring revenue from "governance / compliance" use case (post-IPO module)
- Partnerships provide steady flow (banks/advisors continue advising even in slow market)

**Contingency:** If IPO volume drops >50%, shift to "pre-M&A readiness" positioning; extend TAM.

### Risk 2: Partner Cannibalization

**Risk:** Partners (Big 4, law firms) could build competing tools internally instead of buying IPOReady.

**Mitigation:**
- Lock partners into 2-3 year agreements early
- Co-marketing: make partnership too visible to dismantle
- Revenue share: give partners financial incentive (25-30% better than building in-house)
- Continuous innovation: keep 12-18 months ahead of potential partner tools

**Contingency:** Shift to direct B2B motion; reduce partner dependency to <40% of revenue.

### Risk 3: Competition from Large Vendors

**Risk:** Workiva, Donnelley, or other large firms build IPO module within 18-24 months.

**Mitigation:**
- Speed to market: capture early adopters / mindshare while category is new
- Partnership moat: locked into Big 4 / IB workflows; switching costs high
- Category defense: trademark "IPO readiness," build brand loyalty
- Patents: file 3-5 patents covering novel IPO workflows/compliance tracking

**Contingency:** Become acquisition target (Workiva, Donnelley, financial data vendors); or pivot to downstream services (post-IPO consulting).

### Risk 4: Sales Execution / Hiring

**Risk:** Unable to hire strong sales talent; hiring wrong VP Sales; team churn.

**Mitigation:**
- Hire VP Sales by month 3; backfill if needed by month 6
- Offer meaningful equity (option pool 15-20% total)
- Build culture early: quarterly offsites, transparent strategy, clear commission structure
- Diversify hiring: look beyond traditional SaaS (recruit from law, banking, audit backgrounds)

**Contingency:** Contract first VP Sales from fractional / advisory model; scale to full-time if successful.

### Risk 5: Product-Market Fit Deterioration

**Risk:** Customers churn post-IPO (they graduate); cannot build sticky, recurring revenue.

**Mitigation:**
- Post-IPO module is critical: keep customers paying post-IPO for ongoing compliance
- Target long sales cycles: customers signed for 2-3 year terms during IPO journey
- Net retention >90%: upsell to post-IPO services + new modules

**Contingency:** Build "Private to Public" platform (M&A, venture capital) to expand use cases.
