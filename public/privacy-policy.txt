7. YOUR PRIVACY RIGHTS

7.1 GDPR Rights (EU/EEA Residents)

Right of Access (Article 15)
- Request copy of your personal data
- Understand what data we hold and why
- Response time: 30 days
- Cost: Free (unless manifestly unfounded)

Right to Rectification (Article 16)
- Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
- Update through account settings
- Request manual correction of complex data
- Cost: Free

Right to Erasure (Article 17)
- Request deletion of personal data
- Exceptions: Legal obligation, contract necessity, fraud investigation
- Submit request through account settings
- Response time: 30 days

Right to Restrict Processing (Article 18)
- Limit how we use your data
- Prevents certain processing while we review

Right to Data Portability (Article 20)
- Receive data in structured, machine-readable format (JSON, CSV)
- Transfer data to another service
- Timeline: 30 days

Right to Object (Article 21)
- Object to marketing, profiling, or legitimate interests
- Withdraw consent for any processing

Right to Non-Automated Decision-Making (Article 22)
- Not subject to automated decision-making alone
- PACE assessments include human review available

Right to Lodge Complaint
- File complaint with your Data Protection Authority
- EU: Contact your national DPA

7.2 CCPA Rights (California Residents)

Right to Know (§1798.100)
- Request information we collected
- Request sources of collection
- Request business purpose
- Request recipients of sharing
- Response time: 45 days (extendable 45 days)
- Cost: Free (one per 12 months)

Right to Delete (§1798.105)
- Request deletion of personal information
- Exceptions: Service provision, legal compliance, fraud prevention
- Response time: 45 days

Right to Correct (§1798.100)
- Correct inaccurate personal information
- Available through account settings

Right to Opt-Out (§1798.120)
- Opt-out of "sale" or "sharing" for cross-context advertising
- We do NOT sell personal information
- We do NOT share for cross-context advertising

Right to Limit Use (§1798.121)
- Limit use of sensitive personal information
- Restrict to necessary purposes

Right to Non-Discrimination (§1798.125)
- No denial of service for exercising rights
- No price increase or different terms
- Exception: Financial incentives with opt-in consent

7.3 CPRA Changes (California Privacy Rights Act - Effective 2023)

Additional rights include:
- Right to correct inaccurate data
- Right to delete consumer inferences
- Right to opt-out of automated decision-making
- Enhanced child protections (under 13)
- Enhanced sensitive information protections
- Right to appeal agency decisions

7.4 Other US State Rights

Virginia (VCDPA), Colorado (CPA), Connecticut (CTDPA), Utah (UCPA):
- Right to know, delete, correct, and data portability
- Right to opt-out of targeted advertising

20. DEFINITIONS

"Personal Information" or "Personal Data": Information identifying or relating
to individual (name, email, IP address, device ID, transaction history,
location data, preference inferences).

"Processing": Any operation on personal data (collection, use, storage,
disclosure, deletion, etc.).

"Service Provider" or "Processor": Third party processing personal data on
our behalf under Data Processing Agreement.

"De-identified Data": Information that cannot reasonably identify individual;
stripped of identifiers and aggregated.

"Legitimate Interest": Our business interest balanced against your privacy
rights (when legal basis doesn't apply).

"Consent": Freely given, specific, informed agreement to processing (opt-in;
can be withdrawn).

"Data Breach": Unauthorized access or disclosure of personal information.

"Data Protection Authority": Government agency enforcing privacy laws (CNIL,
ICO, DPA, etc.).

"Sub-processor": Third party used by service provider for personal data
processing (if different from primary processor).
