## The 7-Layer Prediction Engine

### Layer 1: Financial Health Predictor
**Predicts:** Financial statement quality, audit risk, GAAP compliance issues
**Data inputs:**
- Revenue trends (vs. target growth rate)
- Gross margin trends (vs. industry benchmark)
- Operating expense ratio (% of revenue)
- Cash runway (months until burnout)
- Customer churn rate trends
- CAC payback period
- ARR per employee
- Debt/equity ratio
- R&D spending as % of revenue

**Output:** Financial Readiness Score (0-100)
- 85+: IPO-ready financials
- 70-85: Needs 1-2 quarter improvement
- 50-70: Significant work needed
- <50: Not ready for 12+ months

**Example prediction:**
```
Company has:
- Revenue: $10M, growing 35% (Target: 40%)
- Margins: 72% (Industry avg: 68%)
- Runway: 8 months (Min IPO requirement: 12)
- Churn: 1.8% (Good)
- CAC payback: 14 months (Target: <18 months)

Prediction: "Financial readiness will hit 85 in Q3 2026
if revenue growth reaches 38% and runway extends to 12mo."
```

### Layer 2: Regulatory Risk Predictor
**Predicts:** Which regulatory issues will block your IPO and when
**Data inputs:**
- SEC guidance changes (real-time monitoring)
- Industry-specific regulations (healthcare, fintech, etc.)
- International expansion status (GDPR, data residency)
- Data privacy compliance status (CCPA, GDPR)
- Board composition (independent directors, audit committee expertise)
- Conflict of interest disclosures
- Related-party transactions
- Executive insider trading history
- Historical SEC comments on similar companies

**Output:** Regulatory Risk Score + Timeline Impact
- 95-100: No predicted regulatory blockers
- 80-95: Minor issues, fixable in <3 months
- 60-80: Moderate issues, requires 3-6 months remediation
- <60: Major blockers, requires restructuring

**Example prediction:**
```
Company has:
- Audit Committee: All independent ✓
- Board independence: 60% (Target: 66.7%) 
- GDPR status: Compliant ✓
- Insider trading: Clean history ✓
- SEC guidance changes: 2 new rules affect your disclosure ⚠️

Prediction: "Regulatory readiness will hit 95 by June 2026.
You need 1 additional independent board member and updated
risk disclosure in MD&A. Estimated remediation: 2 weeks, zero cost."
```

### Layer 6: Document Risk Intelligence
**Predicts:** Which documents will cause SEC comments/delays

Real-time scanning of your documents for red flags that SEC might challenge:

**Data inputs:**
- Financial statement analysis (revenue recognition, expense timing)
- MD&A text analysis (claims vs. actual metrics)
- Risk factor disclosure (are you missing important risks?)
- Related-party transaction disclosure (complete and fair?)
- Executive compensation disclosure (reasonable vs. peers?)
- Use of proceeds (clear and realistic?)
- Accounting policy disclosure (conservative or aggressive?)

**Output:** Document Risk Score + Likely SEC Comments
```
Your Financial Statements: Risk score 8/10 (low risk)
- Revenue recognition policy: ✓ Conservative
- Expense timing: ✓ Consistent
- Provision for bad debt: ✓ Appropriate

Your MD&A: Risk score 35/10 (HIGH RISK) ⚠️
- Claim: "Fastest-growing company in category"
  Reality: 3 competitors growing faster
  → SEC will flag as misleading
  → Estimated delay: 30 days
  
- Claim: "Gross margins expanding"
  Reality: Margin 76% today, was 74% 2 years ago
  But look: up 8 points vs 65% industry avg
  → Fix: Emphasize "8 points above industry" not "expanding"
  
- Missing risk: "Key customer concentration (32% from 1 customer)"
  → SEC WILL flag as missing disclosure
  → Add: "Loss of this customer would reduce ARR by 32%"

Recommended changes: 3 edits, 2 hours work, saves 45-60 day SEC delay
```

**Data inputs:**

- Red flags that historically led to failed IPOs
- Green flags that historically led to premium valuations

**Output:** Anomaly Report + Implications
```

✓ Revenue growth 42% → Normal (cohort avg: 38%, range: 25-65%)
✓ Gross margin 76% → Good (cohort avg: 68%, top quartile: 75%+)
✓ Customer churn 2.1% → Good (cohort avg: 3.5%)
✓ CAC payback 14 months → Good (cohort avg: 18 months)
⚠️ Operating expense ratio 45% → High (cohort avg: 38%)
  Implication: Spending $4.5M OpEx on $10M revenue
  Is this R&D investment or inefficiency?
  
  If R&D: Can you show product differentiation?
  If inefficiency: Can you cut 10% OpEx without hurting growth?
  
  Recommendation: Calculate R&D as % of revenue (should be 20-30%)
  If you're 15%, you have $0.5M "excess" OpEx to cut

⚠️ Magic number (ARR added / Sales & Marketing spend) = 0.65
  Low (cohort median: 0.85)
  Implication: Your S&M is less efficient than peers
  
  Recommendation: Either improve S&M efficiency or accept lower margins
  Show investors that unit economics will improve at scale (proof: X, Y, Z)

🚨 Customer concentration 32% from 1 customer
  RED FLAG: Cohort average is 8%, max is 25%
  Your company is 4x more concentrated than peers
  
  Implication: IPO investor appetite decreases 20-30%
  Valuation impact: -$15-20M (vs peers at your growth rate)
  
  Recommendation: Diversify revenue
  Action: Set target - get largest customer to <15% within 12 months
  Once achieved, valuation improves $15M

- 1 major red flag (customer concentration)
```
