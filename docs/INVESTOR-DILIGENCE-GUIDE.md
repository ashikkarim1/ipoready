## Data Categories & Timeline

### **TIER 1: Immediate (Day 1 - Signup)**

When a company joins IPOReady, they complete their profile. You get this data immediately.

#### **1.1 Company Fundamentals**

**What You Get:**
- Legal company name
- Incorporation date & jurisdiction
- Business description (2-3 sentences)
- Website & social links
- Founder profiles (name, LinkedIn, background)
- CEO & C-suite team composition
- Number of employees
- Headquarters location + multi-office presence

**Why It Matters:**
- Know who you're investing in before they pitch
- See if founders have prior successful exits (pattern = repeat founder)
- Understand team size growth trajectory
- Confirm they're actually a business (not a side project)

**When You Get It:**
- **Day 1**: Automatically when company signs up

**Update Frequency:**
- **Real-time**: When team changes, new hires, departures

---

#### **1.2 PACE™ Score (IPOReady's Proprietary IPO Readiness Index)**

**What You Get:**
- Overall PACE™ score: 0-100
- Breakdown by dimension:
  - **Governance Readiness** (0-100) — board independence, audit committee, SOX compliance
  - **Compliance Maturity** (0-100) — SOC 2, GDPR/CCPA, regulatory licenses, financial audit status
  - **Financial Readiness** (0-100) — profitability trajectory, unit economics, margin trajectory, cash runway
  - **Market Readiness** (0-100) — TAM size, competitive position, growth trajectory, customer concentration
  - **Growth Trajectory** (0-100) — YoY growth, ARR growth, customer growth, market momentum
- **Estimated IPO Date** — IPOReady's forecast based on PACE progress & historical benchmarks
- **Confidence Level** — how confident is the forecast (80%, 60%, etc.)

**Why It Matters:**
- **PACE replaces 6 months of diligence**
- Shows exactly how IPO-ready a company is
- Lets you forecast exit timing (critical for fund lifecycle planning)
- Early warning: if PACE drops 10 points, company has problems
- Investor-grade metric: all PACE scores are verified by IPOReady's compliance team

**When You Get It:**
- **Day 1**: Generated automatically from company profile
- **Monthly**: Updated as company progresses

**What This Actually Means:**

| PACE Score | Readiness | Timeline |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| 80-100 | Ready to start IPO process | 12-18 months |
| 60-79 | Strong foundation, 1-2 years out | 18-36 months |
| 40-59 | Needs governance/compliance work | 3-5 years |
| 20-39 | Early stage, long runway ahead | 5-10 years |
| <20 | Not yet IPO-ready | 10+ years |

#### **1.3 Risk Assessment**

**What You Get:**
- **Customer Concentration Risk** — if top 3 customers = 60%+ revenue, RED FLAG
- **Churn Risk** — if NRR < 120%, potential issue
- **Regulatory Risk** — outstanding compliance items, pending investigations
- **Market Risk** — market saturation, new competitors, macro headwinds
- **Team Risk** — founder/CTO departures, key person dependencies

**Why It Matters:**
- Identifies risks BEFORE they blow up your investment
- Shows you what to ask in due diligence
- IPOReady's risk team has analyzed 500+ IPO-bound companies
- Pattern recognition: we see risks before investors do

**When You Get It:**
- **Day 1**: Initial risk assessment
- **Monthly**: Updated as company metrics change

**Example:**
```
🔴 CRITICAL: Customer concentration at 52% (top 3 customers)
  → This limits IPO valuation. Public companies rarely exceed 30%.
  → Red flag for churn risk if customer leaves.
  → Action item: ask about customer diversification strategy.

🟡 WARNING: NRR dropped from 135% to 118%
  → Churn increasing. Watch closely.
  → Might indicate market saturation or product issues.

✅ CLEAR: Team stable, no recent departures
```

### **TIER 2: With Raise Documents (Day 5-21)**

When a company launches a fundraise, they upload offering documents. You get comprehensive financial & strategic data.

#### **2.1 Financial Statements & Metrics**

**What You Get:**
- Monthly revenue & bookings (last 24 months minimum)
- Quarterly P&L statements (audited or reviewed)
- Cash flow statement (last 12 months)
- ARR/MRR (Annual/Monthly Recurring Revenue)
- Growth rates: YoY % and QoQ %
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC) by cohort
- Lifetime value (LTV) by customer segment
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR)
- Churn rate (monthly & annual)
- Gross margin %
- Operating margin trajectory
- Current cash balance
- Monthly burn rate
- Runway (months of cash remaining)

**Why It Matters:**
- Shows if the business is actually viable
- NRR > 120% = expansion revenue (VERY good signal)
- CAC < 1 year payback = scalable unit economics
- Positive gross margin + growing = path to profitability
- Cash runway tells you funding urgency (if <12 months, they NEED this raise)

**When You Get It:**
- **Day 5-21**: When company uploads pitch deck & financial model

**Update Frequency:**
- **Monthly**: Most companies share monthly metrics with investors

**Real Example:**
```
DataSync AI (Series B, $15M raise)
- ARR: $5.2M (up from $2.8M YoY) = 85% YoY growth ✅
- NRR: 130% (customers expanding usage) ✅✅
- CAC: $12K, LTV: $78K, LTV:CAC = 6.5x ✅✅✅
- Gross margin: 78% ✅
- Monthly burn: $450K
- Runway: 18 months ✅
- Path to profitability: 24 months
→ Investment Grade: A (strong fundamentals, efficient growth)
```

---

#### **2.2 Customer & Market Data**

**What You Get:**
- Total customer count (by segment: enterprise vs SMB)
- Top 10 customers by revenue (with % of total)
- Customer concentration (what % from top 1, top 3, top 5)
- Monthly / quarterly retention rates
- New logos per month
- Expansion revenue (upsells) vs new logos
- Geographic distribution of revenue
- Product roadmap (12-month view)
- Competitive positioning analysis
- TAM (Total Addressable Market) estimate
- SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market) estimate
- Market share % in served market

**Why It Matters:**
- **Customer concentration > 40% for single customer = RED FLAG**
  - Shows customer dependency risk
  - Limits IPO valuation (public companies must diversify)
  - If that customer leaves, the whole deal falls apart
  
- **High retention (>95%) = sticky product**
  - Proves product-market fit
  - Enables predictable revenue growth
  - De-risks IPO (regulators like predictability)

- **TAM/SAM shows market opportunity**
  - $1B+ TAM = venture-scale opportunity
  - <$500M TAM = challenging for late-stage growth

**When You Get It:**
- **Day 7-21**: With pitch deck and financial model

**Real Example:**
```
PayFlow (Series C, $45M raise)
- Customers: 2,340 (up 45% YoY)
- Top 3 customers: 28% of revenue (GOOD - diversified)
- Retention: 94% (very sticky)
- NRR: 140% (strong expansion)
- TAM: $50B+ (huge market)
- Market share: 0.8% of TAM (massive upside)
→ Market Grade: A+ (huge TAM, sticky product, diversified customer base)
```

#### **2.3 Capitalization Table & Funding History**

**What You Get:**
- All previous funding rounds (date, amount, valuation)
- Current investor list (who invested, stake %, entry valuation)
- Board composition
- Option pool size & vesting details
- Outstanding convertible notes / SAFEs
- Warrant count & exercise prices
- Equity incentives granted to employees
- Full dilution analysis (what happens if all options exercised)
- Down round history (if any)

**Why It Matters:**
- Shows funding momentum (velocity = signal)
- Tells you if previous investors are staying (follow-on investing = confidence)
- Down round = RED FLAG (investors lost money, company struggled)
- High option pool (>20%) = signal of future employee flight risk
- Board composition = who's advising the CEO

**When You Get It:**
- **Day 7-21**: With cap table in pitch deck

**Real Example:**

  - Seed investors: 40%

  - Previous investors: 15% (stayed in, good sign)
  - Employee option pool: 30%

Series B ($15M, $45M post-money): 2023
  - Founders: 23%
  - Series B lead (Bessemer): 25%
  - Accel (follow-on): 12% (confidence signal)
  - Other investors: 10%
  - Employees: 30%

→ Founder dilution is normal & healthy
→ Accel is following on = strong confidence
→ No down rounds = momentum
```

### **TIER 3: IPO Preparation Documents (Day 14-30)**

Once a company is actively preparing for IPO, you get access to deeper strategic documents.

#### **3.1 IPO Readiness Checklist Progress**

**What You Get:**
- Completion status on 180+ IPO milestones:
  - Governance items (board independence, audit committee, compensation committee, etc.)
  - Financial audit readiness (auditor selected, audit plan)
  - Disclosure documents (10-K, 10-Q readiness)
  - Internal controls (SOX 404 compliance)
  - Legal/IP (patent clearance, contract review)
  - Regulatory (exchange requirements, SEC feedback)
  - Underwriter selection (banking relationships)
  
- For each milestone:
  - Completion percentage (0-100%)
  - Target date
  - Owner (who's responsible)
  - Dependencies (what needs to happen first)
  - Issues or blockers

**Why It Matters:**
- Shows EXACT progress toward IPO
- Highlights bottlenecks (what's holding them back)
- Predicts IPO timing with accuracy (if they're 80% done, IPO is 18-24 months out)
- De-risks your investment (you can see if they're actually moving)

**When You Get It:**
- **Day 14-30**: When company starts active IPO prep
- **Weekly**: Updated as company progresses

**Real Example:**
```
DataSync AI IPO Readiness (Updated: Jan 15, 2026)

GOVERNANCE: 85% complete
  ✅ Board independence policy adopted
  ✅ Audit committee established
  ✅ Compensation committee established
  🟡 Internal audit function (in progress, due Feb 28)
  ✅ Code of ethics adopted

FINANCIAL: 95% complete
  ✅ Big 4 audit firm selected (Deloitte)
  ✅ FY2025 audit in progress
  ✅ SOX 404 readiness assessment complete
  
DISCLOSURE: 40% complete

#### **3.2 Underwriter & Advisor Pipeline**

**What You Get:**
- Lead underbank(s) (if selected)
- Secondary underbanks
- Legal counsel (company & underwriter)
- Auditor
- Investor relations advisor
- Underwriter fee structure
- IPO size target (preliminary range)
- Expected IPO timing
- List of potential lock-up periods

**Why It Matters:**
- **Lead underwriter choice = major signal**
  - Goldman Sachs leads = mega unicorn (>$10B exit)
  - Morgan Stanley/JPMorgan = $3-10B exit
  - Mid-market banks = $1-3B exit
- Shows how serious they are (hiring good counsel = committed)
- IPO size tells you valuation expectations

**When You Get It:**
- **Day 21-60**: When company selects banking team

**Real Example:**
```
PayFlow IPO Banking Team
- Lead Underwriter: Goldman Sachs
- Secondary: Morgan Stanley, JP Morgan
- Company Counsel: Cooley LLP
- Auditor: PwC
- IR Advisor: Abernathy MacGregor

→ Goldman leading = expecting $5B+ IPO
→ PwC audit = institutional quality
→ This team doesn't do sub-$1B deals
```
